Lesson

Definition

Sound are pressure waves in a medium (usually the air). It's frequency describes how many of these waves occur in one second. Audio Frequency is measured in hertz (hz). One herz is equivalent to one wave per second.

Hearing Range

You may have realised while playing around with the slider, that you can only hear the frequencies in the middle of it. There are several reasons for this:

  1. The range of frequencies humans can hear is about 20 to 20000hz.
  2. Your audio equipment has a certain frequency response.
  3. 0hz means there is no sound, so you can not hear anything there.

Frequency spectrum

Frequency spectrums are usually drawn on a logarithmic scale. Example

The button below generates two tones. The frequency of the first one is determined by the value in the text box next to it. The second one is played at the given frequency plus 100hz. Try a couple of frequencies.

Hearing the differences between these two tones gets more and more difficult, the higher the base frequency is. In other words, if the frequency is high, a small change is hard to hear. If the frequency is low small differences are easy to hear. This connection was first described by Ernst Heinrich Weber. According to Wikipedia:

Weber's law states that the just-noticeable difference between two stimuli is proportional to the magnitude of the stimuli.
Therefore the notes corresponding to low frequencies are all nearer to each other (frequency wise) than the notes corresponding to the higher frequencies.
